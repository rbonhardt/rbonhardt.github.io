Have you always wondered about this mystical world of websites and how to make them?

Or thought it would be too hard to learn?

Or just don’t know where to start?

Well don’t worry, soon (less than 20 hours) you will laugh in the face of CSS and HTML and be on the way to learning bigger and better things.

The goal of this article is to take a person completely new to building websites (HTML and CSS) and to have their first site up in less  a day.  From their you will have the groundwork and fundamentals to continue trying new things and building any site you want.

This is the article I wish I had when I decided learn web programming 4 years ago.  I searched the internet for the fastest way to learn html, css, and javascript.  Instead what I should have done is scoured the internet for skill acquisition models and meta-learning principles.  From my findings my plan would have been reversed.

Instead of taking the time to learn each – html, css, and javascript – in their entirety I would have attacked it along the guidelines of “The First 20 Hours”.  So here’s to you, person excited and scared to jump into the world of  front-end web development:

Note: In case you're confused, front-end is everything you see as a visitor to the site (HTML is the structure, CSS is the style).  The more complicated stuff is the back-end - stuff behind the scenes - which is the functionality.  For now don't worry about any of that.

First off, what do you want to be able to do? Be specific.

 Answer this question so you have a measuring stick and goal in mind.  With this goal you will be more inclined to work towards it and know when you have achieved it.

Are you trying to learn because you have a business idea and want to test it? 

If its just one item (ebook idea, service, ecourse) then use unbounce or instapage to create your site (which is actually just a page) – no coding necessary ; you’d just be wasting hours when you could skip to testing your business idea.  To accept payments on those sites you can use the simple, new and fastly-growing gumroad (great for digital goods, not physical….yet), the old standard paypal or one of my favorites wepay.

But if you’re in this category STOP. 

Here’s a hint on how to avoid losing $15,000 on your first business idea like I did.  TEST YOUR IDEA FIRST.  Before you jump in creating a website, buying product, business cards, tshirts, trademarks, web hosting and taking hours to set up your business. STOP

Take a couple dollars and couple hours and check out one or two of the following:

  2. How to Make your First Dollar: course from the guys at AppSumo on how to test your business ideas and get your first dollar.  QUALITY.  If you don't agree you get your money refunded
  3. Make your First Dollar: a hyphenated course from above on Skillshare.  Quick and Easy, but powerful.
  4. The $100 Startup
  5. Or the automating section of Four Hour Workweek.

You will learn how to test your ideas the right way before you waste money. You can send me a thank you card later.

Do you want to start a blog or virtual site to promote yourself?

Here are some great blogging platforms that require no coding knowledge (in the order I recommend): wordpress.com, weebly, and squarespace.

Do you want to build and design websites for clients?

The first question from here is what type of functionality are they going to need.

If it’s a basic site with just content and minimal functionality like a form then I recommend going with weebly or squarespace and building the site.  You can find plugins from 3rd party services and use weebly’s custom html and css boxes to add those plugins in.  For example: for a form you can create one in wufoo and then add it in weebly.

If your client’s site is going to need more functionality (bells and whistles) then lets learn some CSS and HTML.  More to come on that….

Do you want to one day be able to build sites from scratch and/or move on to actual web application development?

Well then…welcome to the fun stuff and obviously you will have to learn HTML and CSS.

So we begin...

Our lovable project is going to be our first website

Remember this is just your first site and you’re acquiring the skills of front-end web development in the fastest way possible.  So you’re going to jump in over your head, take shortcuts, experiment, and create fast feedback loops (all of these ideas come from The First 20 Hours).  The cool thing is that web development is a perfect environment for all the above.

A set of subskills

Barebones front-end web development is composed of 3 subskills: design, css, and html.  I won’t hit on design here, but less is more; design minimal.
